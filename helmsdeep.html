Behind the Scenes — Making of Caddyshack
Bill Murray improvised almost everything. The famous "Cinderella story" scene was entirely unscripted — he just grabbed a rake and started narrating into it. Harold Ramis let the cameras roll.
Ted Knight and Bill Murray reportedly did NOT get along. Knight was a classically trained actor who liked hitting his marks and sticking to the script. Murray thought the script was a suggestion at best.
Murray was only supposed to have a small supporting role. He showed up on set, started improvising, and basically took over every scene he was in. Nobody stopped him.
The gopher was originally a minor background gag. Test audiences loved it so much they added more gopher footage in post-production, including the famous dancing scene to "I'm Alright" by Kenny Loggins.
The film was shot almost entirely at the Grande Oaks Golf Club (now called Rolling Hills) in Davie, Florida — not at an actual prestigious country club.
Chevy Chase and Bill Murray had exactly ONE scene together. The entire "Be the ball, Danny. Be the ball." and joint-smoking sequence was shot in one afternoon. They had no other scenes together in the whole film.
Writer/producer Doug Kenney (National Lampoon co-founder) died tragically in 1980, shortly after the film's release, never knowing how beloved it would become.
The production was described as total chaos. Drugs, parties, and complete disorganization. Harold Ramis later called it "the most disorganized shoot I was ever on." It grossed over M and became one of the most quoted comedies of all time.
