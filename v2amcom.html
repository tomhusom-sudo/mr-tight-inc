Hospital DBs

Twenty-five years ago I walked into a company called Amcom. Jack Collins ran the place. I found a guy named Chad Belmer, trained him on the database, and somewhere along the way we became great friends. He worked under me. Then he became my boss. Then he left. Then I became the boss and hired him back. We spent 23 years together building something — from Amcom to SPOK — and we weren't done yet.

We're talking hundreds of crappy hospital databases. Government systems held together with duct tape and prayer. Ancient Word docs. Handwritten notes — yes, actual handwritten notes — that somehow needed to live in a database. HL7 patient data. HIPAA certifications. ETL pipelines pulling data from places that had no business being called a data source.

And when it broke — and it always broke — there was no hiding. Systems down. Customers screaming. CEO standing over your shoulder at 2am sweating bullets while you're on-call trying to hold the whole thing together with your bare hands.

We lived that. For 23 years.

Today we're starting V2 Amcom. We are a data company built for the future — powered by AI and the tools that actually work today. We've seen the worst data imaginable and we cleaned it up. We integrate data, deploy AI tools, and send agentic agents to work — helping companies around the world get their house in order before the AI future arrives and leaves them behind.

AI is only as good as the data you feed it. Dirty data, scattered data, ignored data — it'll sink you. Clean data wins. Organized data wins. That's what we do. We get your data ready so you can compete in the world that's already here.
