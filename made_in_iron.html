About the Artist
Kelly Glaser

Kelly Glaser has dabbled in plenty of artforms, but his favorite is also the most unique. In a garage in Aberdeen, South Dakota, he builds sculptures from scrap metal and discarded parts. Because of what he works with, no two pieces are ever alike.

"I look at my junk pieces and figure it out from there. It's more fun to decide on the way. I always want to try something different."

A driver for Spee-Dee Delivery by day, Glaser finds a lot of his inspiration out on the routes — always on the lookout for new materials, asking to go through scrap bins. People sometimes drop off their junk in exchange for a finished piece.

"Finding stuff that fits well into the sculpture is the best part. I like to take something that's been used up and has served its purpose and give it some new life."

His sculptures range from small lawn decorations all the way up to a full 6-foot tall robot. He alternates between big and small pieces to keep it interesting. Small work runs about three hours; some builds have gone past fifteen.

⚙ The Workshop ⚙
Kelly Glaser · Made N Iron

Before the sculptures, Glaser studied commercial art at Northern State University and spent about five years as a tattoo artist. He took welding classes in high school, spotted a cheap welder on Facebook Marketplace one day, and the rest is history.

"The first thing I made was a tiny snake, and I built up from there. Although I'm not really a welder — I just make stuff stick together."

Since he started welding, his yard has gradually turned into its own museum. He sells his pieces at Arts in the Park and craft fairs all over the region.
