The Todd Father Weighs In

lookslikelarry.com

A professional critique of LooksLikeLarry Productions — pin-up poster revivalists, custom car photographers, and apparently Todd's problem now.

3.2
Verdict
out of 10

I have reviewed this website with the full weight of my authority and a strong cup of coffee. What I found was a brave attempt — and I use the word "brave" the way you'd describe someone who orders the fish at an airport — technically a choice, but raises real questions about judgment.

— The Todd Father, Site Critic Emeritus
⚖️
Formal Charges
The Indictment
  • COUNT I
    The Name
    lookslikelarry.com — a domain name that sounds like something you'd whisper to someone at a funeral. "Psst... lookslikelarry." Nobody knows who Larry is. Nobody asked. The name implies a resemblance to a Larry, which raises the question: is the site itself Larry? Are we Larry? Has Larry been inside us all along?
  • COUNT II
    "Bringing Back the Pin-Up Poster"
    Larry, with respect — pin-up posters never left. They have been continuously available at Spencer's Gifts since 1987 and remain there today, sandwiched between the body glitter and the shot glasses with sayings on them. You are not reviving a lost art. You are selling a calendar.
  • COUNT III
    The Pricing Structure
    $149 to reserve a photoshoot. Not for the photoshoot. To reserve it. That is $149 for the privilege of giving Larry your calendar availability. Meanwhile, a $79 custom car poster gets you a pin-up model next to your vehicle — which, coincidentally, is also what happens when you park near a dealership on a Saturday. Free of charge.
  • COUNT IV
    The Design Philosophy
    White background. Arial font. Dark gray buttons. This is not a design — this is what happens when you dismiss every suggestion in Squarespace's onboarding wizard. The aesthetic can be summarized as "someone installed a WordPress theme and then left for lunch and never came back." The retro pin-up concept deserves something bold. What it got was a dental office website.
  • COUNT V
    The Stickers
    $12 pin-up stickers. Stickers are the merchandise you sell when you're not entirely sure you have a real business yet. They're the "we'll figure it out" of the product catalog. Larry is selling $12 stickers on a site that also promises to revolutionize the pin-up industry. These two facts cannot coexist peacefully.
  • COUNT VI
    The Country Selector
    There is a country selector in the footer. Larry is going international. Larry, who is selling $12 stickers and charging $149 to talk about scheduling, has prepared for a global audience. One imagines customs officials in 47 countries opening packages and finding a pin-up calendar from a man named Larry. Bold. Visionary. Bewildering.
  • COUNT VII
    The Testimonials
    The site features customer testimonials praising Larry's professionalism and quality. Larry is using his own website to tell you that people like him. This is the digital equivalent of putting your own name on your trophy. The testimonials may be genuine. The Todd Father cannot verify this. The Todd Father is suspicious.
The Ruling

LooksLikeLarry Productions is found guilty on all seven counts. The sentence is a mandatory website redesign, the immediate retirement of the Arial font, and a serious conversation with someone — anyone — about what the word "branding" means.

That said, The Todd Father acknowledges that Larry is out here doing something. He has a domain. He has products. He has a country selector for reasons that remain unclear. In the grand tradition of American hustle, Larry looked at the pin-up poster market and said "I want in." You cannot fully hate that.

You can, however, hate the font. The font is Arial. There is no excuse.
